More about Peace at Pocket

Peace a Pocket is a project made for those struggling with mental health and self-acceptance. We are responsible for providing a safe, comfortable enviroment for all our users, giving them the guarantee that they'll have the best possible professionals to help them trough their personal journeys

Our main difference is the variety of places we offer for our users to stay during their treatments. From small towns in South America to big cities in Europe, we give users the possibility to choose where they want to spend their healing process

We also have a very diverse option of mental health professionals, available in more than five different languages, on all continents

Treatments

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

CBT is a widely used psychotherapy approach for treating anxiety disorders. It focuses on identifying and changing negative thought patterns and behaviors that contribute to anxiety. Through structured sessions, individuals learn coping strategies, relaxation techniques, and problem-solving skills to manage anxiety symptoms effectively.

Medication

Several medications are used to treat anxiety disorders, including selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRIs), serotonin-norepinephrine reuptake inhibitors (SNRIs), benzodiazepines, and beta-blockers. SSRIs and SNRIs are commonly prescribed as first-line treatments due to their effectiveness in reducing anxiety symptoms over time. Benzodiazepines are fast-acting but are typically used short-term due to their potential for dependence and withdrawal symptoms.

Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR)

MBSR is a mindfulness-based intervention that combines meditation, yoga, and mindfulness practices to reduce stress and anxiety. By cultivating present-moment awareness and non-judgmental acceptance, individuals can learn to respond to anxiety triggers more skillfully and reduce overall anxiety levels.

Exposure Therapy

Exposure therapy is a behavioral therapy approach used to treat specific phobias and anxiety disorders such as PTSD. It involves gradually exposing individuals to feared situations or stimuli in a controlled and safe environment. Through repeated exposure, individuals can learn to confront and manage their anxiety responses, leading to reduced fear and avoidance behaviors.

Lifestyle Modifications

Making lifestyle changes can also help alleviate anxiety symptoms. Regular exercise, adequate sleep, healthy diet, and stress-management techniques like deep breathing, progressive muscle relaxation, and guided imagery can all contribute to reducing anxiety levels. Additionally, minimizing caffeine and alcohol intake, establishing a routine, and engaging in enjoyable activities can promote overall well-being and resilience against anxiety.

Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation (TMS)

TMS is a non-invasive procedure that uses magnetic fields to stimulate nerve cells in the brain. It is primarily used to treat major depressive disorder (MDD) that has not responded adequately to antidepressant medications. TMS targets specific regions of the brain associated with mood regulation and has shown effectiveness in reducing depressive symptoms.

Light Therapy

Light therapy, also known as phototherapy, is commonly used to treat seasonal affective disorder (SAD) and certain types of depression. It involves exposure to bright light that mimics natural sunlight, which can help regulate circadian rhythms and improve mood. Light therapy is often used in conjunction with other treatments such as psychotherapy or medication.

Electroconvulsive Therapy (ECT)

ECT is a medical procedure that involves passing electrical currents through the brain to induce controlled seizures. It is typically used for severe cases of depression, bipolar disorder, or other mood disorders that have not responded to other treatments. ECT is considered safe and effective, particularly for individuals who require rapid symptom relief or cannot tolerate medications.

Herbal Supplements and Nutritional Therapy

Some individuals with mood disorders may benefit from herbal supplements and nutritional interventions. For example, St. John's Wort is a popular herbal remedy for mild to moderate depression. Omega-3 fatty acids, found in fish oil supplements, have also been studied for their potential mood-stabilizing effects. Additionally, ensuring a balanced diet with adequate nutrients can support overall mental health.

Acupuncture
Acupuncture is a traditional Chinese medicine technique that involves inserting thin needles into specific points on the body to restore energy flow and balance. Some studies suggest that acupuncture may have a positive impact on mood disorders by influencing neurotransmitter levels, reducing stress hormones, and promoting relaxation. However, more research is needed to fully understand its effectiveness in treating mood disorders.
Art Therapy

Art therapy involves using artistic activities such as drawing, painting, sculpting, or crafting to express emotions, reduce stress, and enhance self-awareness. Engaging in creative processes can be therapeutic, allowing individuals to explore their feelings, gain insights, and find relaxation through artistic expression. Art therapy can be particularly beneficial for those struggling with stress-related symptoms or burnout.

Mind-Body Practices (e.g., Tai Chi, Qigong)

Mind-body practices combine physical movements with mental focus and relaxation techniques to harmonize the mind and body. Tai Chi and Qigong, for example, incorporate gentle, flowing movements, deep breathing, and mindfulness practices. These practices can improve balance, flexibility, resilience to stress, and overall mental well-being. They are often recommended as complementary approaches to traditional stress management strategies.

Massage Therapy

Massage therapy involves hands-on manipulation of muscles and soft tissues to promote relaxation, reduce muscle tension, and alleviate physical symptoms of stress. Techniques such as Swedish massage, deep tissue massage, and aromatherapy massage can help improve circulation, release endorphins (natural painkillers and mood enhancers), and create a sense of calmness and rejuvenation.

Music Therapy

Music therapy utilizes the therapeutic properties of music to reduce stress and promote emotional well-being. Listening to calming music, playing musical instruments, or engaging in group music therapy sessions can help individuals relax, express emotions, and find comfort during stressful times. Music therapy is often used in healthcare settings but can also be beneficial for anyone experiencing stress or burnout.

Nature Therapy (Ecotherapy)
Nature therapy involves spending time in natural environments, such as parks, forests, or gardens, to improve mental well-being and reduce stress. Research suggests that exposure to nature can lower cortisol levels (the stress hormone), enhance mood, and promote relaxation. Activities like nature walks, gardening, or simply enjoying outdoor settings can be beneficial for managing stress and preventing burnout.
